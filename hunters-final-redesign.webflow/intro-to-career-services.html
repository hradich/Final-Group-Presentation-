Milestone Develop Your Resume

This assignment is required if you are seeking career assistance

Background

Your resume or CV is your chance to impress an employer with your skills and accomplishments. An employer-competitive resume or CV focuses on results, uses dynamic language, and appears organized, clean, and free of mistakes.

Objectives

  • Develop a draft of your resume or CV
  • Submit your resume for feedback

How to Update your Resume

Step 1. Update Contact Details
  • Include full name, email, phone number, email address (not Hotmail, Yahoo, AOL, or School).
  • Your address, including city & state, zip code.
  • Links to your LinkedIn & Portfolio and a link to your portfolio website.
Step 2. Summary Section
  • Title that is industry specific. 
  • Background experience that directly correlates to your target role. 
  • Avoid the use of pronouns. (less... I, me, he, she, more… We, our team, role specific distinction) 
Step 3. List 2-3 transferable skills.
  • Ex. adaptable, time management, communication, innovative, collaborative, etc.
  • The number of years of related experience If new to the field, be strategic about listing your years of experience.
  • Accomplishments, recognitions, or awards.
  • Education, Training, or certifications( List Schools + Boot Camp dates)
Step 4: Next Detail and structure skills in different ways.
  • Interaction
  • UX Design Methods
  • Graphic Design
  • Coding
  • Prototyping Tools
Step 5: Projects
  • Up to 3 of your strongest projects, with a brief description, technologies used, and shareable links (if applicable).
Step 6. Work Experience
  • Experience listed in reverse chronological order, with the job title, job description, company name, city & state, and employment dates. Avoid adding experience prior to 10 years from the current date.
  • Start every bullet with an action verb; don’t use the same verb more than once. 
  • Experience listed in reverse chronological order, with the job title, job description, company name, city & state, and employment dates. Avoid adding experience prior to 10 years from the current date.
  • Bullets are concise, direct, and listed in order of importance. 
Step 7: Education
  • List education briefly at the beginning if you do not have much experience or relevant degrees. Otherwise, list education at the end of the resume. 
  • Includes Boot Camp as the most recent item in education, listed as a Certificate.

Submission:

Go to the Career Services tab in BCS. Select ‘Milestone: Resume’ to submit your resume.
